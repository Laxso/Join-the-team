Hey, You Wanna Be a Background Game Dev?

Hey there! I’m Travis K. Nagel, the lead manager, scriptwriter, and programmer for an exciting visual novel project currently in development. Our dedicated team is working hard to bring this game to life. We’re aiming to launch the game on Steam by fall 2026! We’re on the lookout for a digital background designer who’s familiar with visual novels. If you have a portfolio showcasing your work, we’d love to see it! To give you an idea of the process we’re looking for, it’s similar to what’s outlined in the references. Even if you don’t have much experience with visual novel backgrounds but have completed work in architecture or general background design, that’s perfectly fine. We’d much rather see polished work outside our niche than incomplete projects that closely match our vision. This is mostly a volunteer position, perfect if you’re looking to gain real-world experience with a passionate and supportive team. While I’d love to offer payment, we’re currently working with a tight budget that is funded entirely out of pocket. We meet bi-weekly online, and we’re super flexible with deadlines. We understand the importance of balancing work, school, and life, and we’re happy to accommodate your schedule. If this sounds like something you’d love to be part of, hit me up on Instagram or email me. We’d be thrilled to hear from you!

Hey, You Wanna Be a Character Game Dev?

Good day, future character game dev! My name is Travis K. Nagel, and we’re currently looking for a character artist who’s interested in expanding their portfolio, whether that’s in game development or simply building a strong art showcase. For character designs, we’re looking for someone comfortable working on chibi art, character sprites, and character scenes or events. Our preferred style is anime-inspired, similar to what’s shown on our Progress Page, since the project is a visual novel. That said, if you have your own unique anime-inspired style, we’d love to see it too! We’re open to different approaches that still fit within the slice-of-life, drama, and romance tone of the story. Ideally, we’re aiming for a cute and expressive art style that captures the heart of those genres.
